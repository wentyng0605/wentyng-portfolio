The Multidisciplinary Portfolio Hub

Role: UI/UX Designer & Full-Stack Developer

The Challenge: Balancing Divergent Identities

My first portfolio was built on Strikingly, but I was limited by its single-page structure and paywalled features. I challenged this by migrating to a custom Vanilla Tech Stack, granting me the architectural freedom to build a multi-dimensional experience while achieving 100% control over my digital identity. Most traditional templates force a singular focus; I needed to challenge this status quo by creating a high-performance system that remains intuitive despite the complexity of the content.

The Approach: Engineering for Performance & Storytelling

Challenging the CMS Status Quo: Moving away from heavy, script-bloated CMS frameworks, I built this platform from the ground up using a Vanilla Tech Stack. By bypassing third-party overhead, I secured optimized Core Web Vitals and total granular control over the UI's behavior, proving that high-end design should never come at the cost of technical efficiency.

Information Architecture: To prevent cognitive overload, I engineered a modular 6-sector system. Each domain uses a unique theme color (e.g., Pink for Animation, Blue for UI/UX) to provide immediate sub-conscious navigation cues.

Design Decisions & HCI Implementation

Context-Aware Navigation

Developed a custom vertical nav-progress tracker using the Intersection Observer API, providing users with constant orientation within long-scroll case studies.

Immersive Canvas Logic

Utilized a deep-themed background as a "neutral canvas" to minimize visual noise, allowing high-fidelity project renders to remain the primary focus.

Performance-First Assets

Implemented lazy-loading and optimized media delivery to ensure that heavy 3D video content doesn't compromise the site's Core Web Vitals.

Responsive Fluidity

Built a custom grid system that adapts from 4K ultra-wide monitors to mobile devices, ensuring the visual hierarchy remains intact regardless of screen size.

UX Case Study & System Design

Bloom-In-Motion: Pioneering Campus Digital Ticketing

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer & Researcher

The Challenge: From Manual Chaos to Systemic Innovation

The idea was born when a friend, serving as an event chairperson, faced immense frustration with the university's "Paper-Era" workflows. Her department relied on fragmented messaging and manual spreadsheets, causing high cognitive load and frequent booking errors. I challenged this inefficient status quo—which no other society had addressed before—to prove that even campus-scale events deserve professional-grade digital systems.

The Process: Bridging Organizer Needs with User Ease

My primary goal was to transition the organization to a "Digital-First" workflow, focusing on two key pillars: Operational Accuracy and User Efficiency. I focused on reducing the complex offline process into a streamlined digital experience of under 3 minutes.

To overcome "Form Fatigue," I implemented Progressive Disclosure, breaking the high-input process (seat selection & payment) into logical, digestible chunks to prevent mobile user drop-off.

Design Strategy & HCI Implementation

Fitts’s Law in Action

Optimized touch-targets for the interactive seat map, ensuring even users on small mobile monitors could pick seats accurately without frustration.

Visibility of System Status

Applying Nielsen’s Heuristics, I built visual feedback loops for seat reservations and payments, providing the "immediate closure" necessary for building user trust.

Cognitive Load Reduction

Re-engineered the information hierarchy to eliminate "payment tracking fatigue," replacing manual verification with a centralized digital dashboard.

Pioneering Impact

As the first society to implement such a system, we set a new benchmark for campus event management, mirroring the standards of large-scale commercial events.

Outcome & Measurable Impact

The project successfully supported the 2024 University Dance Showcase, proving that well-designed UX can eliminate long-standing operational bottlenecks.

UI/UX Case Study

TravelGo: Redesign Travel Apps

Role: UI/UX Designer

The Challenge

Travelers often struggle to balance itinerary logistics with budget constraints. My goal for this HCI project was to design a seamless management solution that integrates hotel/flight booking with real-time budget tracking, validated through rigorous user testing.

User Research & Insights

Conducted quantitative research with 25 participants to measure usability and user satisfaction.

User Demographic: Target audience aged 18-25, focusing on tech-savvy frequent travelers.

Testing Setup: Within-Subject Design where users performed tasks (Login, Explore Navigation, Feedback) to test intuitiveness.

Design Strategy & Heuristic Evaluation

Leveraging Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics, I ensured the interface feels familiar and reliable.

Recognition Over Recall

Clear iconography and visual cues reduce cognitive load. Users recognize actions instead of remembering locations.

System Match to Real World

Familiar gestures (pinch-to-zoom, drag) and logical booking flow mirror real-world sequences.

Consistency & Standards

Strict visual language across the app ensures users feel confident navigating.

The Results & Discussion

The prototype was evaluated using the System Usability Scale (SUS) for quantitative validation.
