Part I · The Overview

Gameplay & Production

Project Overview

The Little Guardian is a 3D side-scrolling platformer-adventure set in a medieval, mysterious world. The player takes on the role of a young guardian tasked with uncovering the cause of a dark curse that is draining the life from the forest. By blending platforming and RPG elements, the game invites players to journey through a semi-open world filled with secrets, puzzles, and challenges. As the mystery of the kingdom unfolds, the player must master movement, solve puzzles, and battle enemies to confront the spreading curse at the heart of the decay.

Part IV · The Research

Academic Thesis

01.

Research Focus: Does Agency Mechanics Bring Engagement?

Does more freedom equal more fun? This thesis quantifies the relationship between Mechanical Agency and User Engagement. Using a mix of quantitative surveys (n=98) and qualitative playtests (n=39), I designed a game prototype that successfully demonstrates how tailoring mechanics to specific player archetypes (Action vs. Adventure) can statistically improve the user experience.

02.

The Research Foundation: Behind The Framework

Aim

To investigate the relationship between player trait preferences, Actual Mechanical Agency (AMA) mechanics, and player engagement in Platformer-Adventure games.

Objectives

01

To identify the top five game mechanics most preferred by Aesthetic-Goal and Aesthetic-Challenge players in a Platformer-Adventure game using preliminary analysis.

02

To develop a Platformer-Adventure game prototype incorporating the selected game mechanics.

03

To evaluate players' perceived AMA using the Game Sense of Agency (GSoA) and analyse its relationship with player engagement using the Game Engagement Questionnaire (GEQ).

04

To analyse the differences in preferences for AMA-mechanics between Aesthetic-Goal and Aesthetic-Challenge players in the developed prototype.

Research Questions

RQ1

What are the top five game mechanics most preferred by Aesthetic-Goal and Aesthetic-Challenge players in a Platformer-Adventure game?

RQ2

How does perceived Actual Mechanical Agency (AMA) impact player engagement among Aesthetic-Goal and Aesthetic-Challenge players?

RQ3

What are the differences in the preferences of Aesthetic-Goal and Aesthetic-Challenge players with the top five AMA-mechanics in the proposed game?

Core Finding

While Exploration is universally valued, AG players lean towards intellectual engagement, whereas AC players demand fluid control and environmental reactivity.

Key Highlights

  • Empowerment Drives Retention: Higher Mechanical Agency (AMA) statistically increases overall player engagement.
  • Tailored Experiences: AG players prefer progression-based mechanics like Collectible Items (p = 0.006).
  • Movement is Key: AC players prioritise exploration efficiency and Movement Aids for better flow.
